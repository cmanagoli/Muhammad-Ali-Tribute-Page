Muhammad Ali

Greatest boxer of all time

Timeline of Ali's life:

  • 1942 - Born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr., in Louisville, Kentucky. He was a descendant of slaves of the antebellum South.
  • 1954 - Grew up with dyslexia among racial segregation. He was once denied drinking water at a store because of his skin color. Has his bike stolen at the age of 12 and encounters police officer and boxing coach Joe E. Martin. Ali tells Martin that he is going to "whup" the thief to which Joe replies "You better learn how to box first."
  • 1954 - Wins his first amateur boxing match against Ronnie O'Keefe by way of split decision.
  • 1960 - Wins gold medal in heavyweight division at Summer Olympics in Rome.
  • 1960 - Makes professional debut against Tunney Hunsaker and wins six-round decision.
  • 1964 - Becomes number one contender for heavyweight title match against Sonny Liston. Sonny Liston is a dominating fighter with an intimidating personality and a criminal past with ties to the mob. Ali is a 7-1 underdog. Defeats Liston by TKO after 6 rounds and is crowned Heavyweight Champion of the World.
  • 1965 - Defeats Sonny Liston in a rematch. Knocks him out in the very first round. Entire fight lasts for less than 2 minutes.
  • 1966 - Ali refuses to be inducted into the armed forces. Systematically denied a boxing license in every state and then gets stripped of his passport.
  • 1966-70 - Did not fight during his physical peak from the ages of 25 to 29 as his case worked its way through the appeals process in court. During this time, Ali spoke at colleges across the nation, criticizing the Vietnam war and advocating African-American pride and racial justice.
  • 1970 - Returns to the ring and defeats Jerry Quarry and Oscar Bonavena to become number one contender for world heavyweight title.
  • 1971 - Ali loses by unanimous decision to Joe Frazier, his first professional defeat.
  • 1974 - Defeats Joe Frazier by unanimous decision to secure contendership for title which was now held by the notorious George Foreman.
  • 1974 - Defeats George Foreman by knockout at the end of the 8th round in Kinshasa, Zaire (Democratic Republic of Congo). Becomes two-time Heavyweight Champion of the World.
  • 1984 - Diagnosed with Parkinson's disease resulting from head trauma during his years as a boxer. It is estimated that he took around 29,000 punches during his lifetime.
  • 2016 - Ali succumbed to complications from Parkinson's disease. He died at the age of 74.
